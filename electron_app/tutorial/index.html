CHAPTER 01

Registration & Setup

Create your account, set up your business profile, choose your industry, and select the features your business needs.

Creating Your Account

When you launch Zeebroo POS for the first time, you'll see the login screen. Click Create account to begin the 4-step registration wizard.

1 Account Credentials
Enter your Email address and create a secure Password. These will be your login credentials for every device running Zeebroo POS. Click Next.
2 Profile Information
Enter your Full name and your Business name. Your business name will appear on receipts, reports, and throughout the app. Click Next.
3 Choose Your Industry
Select the category that best describes your business — Local Retail, Food & Beverage, Restaurant, Bar, Healthcare, and more. Selecting Restaurant or Bar automatically enables the Restaurant feature. Click Next.
4 Select Features
Toggle the features your business needs. Each feature unlocks a dedicated tab in the ribbon. Restaurant and Point of Sale are mutually exclusive — choose the one that fits your setup. Click Create Account to finish.
Tip You can always change your enabled features later from Home → Tools → Feature Management. Changes take effect after the app restarts.

Available Features

Select only what your business uses — each enabled feature adds a tab to the ribbon interface.

Point of Sale
Cashier checkout, product search, cart management, receipts, and customer accounts.
Product Management
Product catalog, variants, categories, brands, discounts, and barcode printing.
Stock Management
Track inventory levels, run stock audits, manage purchase orders and GRNs.
Bill Management
Supplier bills, loans, rentals, properties, and expense tracking.
Human Resources
Employee records, departments, salary, payroll, and attendance.
Restaurant
Table management, menu items, orders, KDS, ingredients, and restaurant POS. Cannot be combined with Point of Sale.
Services
Service-bound products and job management for service businesses.
Social Campaigns
Design studio for creating branded social media content and ad creatives.

Signing In to an Existing Account

1
Enter your email and password

On the login screen, type your registered email and password.

2
Click Sign In

The app will authenticate with the server and load your business data. The main interface opens automatically.

3
Set up a bank account (first time only)

If you haven't added a bank account yet, the Bank Account Setup Wizard will open. Add at least one account so payments can be settled correctly.

1 Add Bank Account
Choose your bank from the list or enter a custom name. Select the account type (Current, Savings, Cash, etc.) and assign a currency.
2 Account Details
Enter the Account number and any additional reference details. These are used for reconciliation and reporting only.
3 Opening Balance
Enter the current balance of this account. This sets the starting point for all financial tracking. Click Save to complete the setup.

Switching Businesses or Branches

If your account is linked to multiple businesses or branches, click your profile name in the top-right corner to open the account panel. Use Switch Business or Switch Branch to change context. All data refreshes automatically.

CHAPTER 02

Home Dashboard

Your command centre — daily KPIs, sales trends, quick actions, and real-time activity feed.

The Ribbon Interface

Zeebroo POS uses a Microsoft Office–style ribbon at the top of the screen. It's organised into tabs (Home, POS, Sales, Inventory, etc.) and groups within each tab. Click a tab to switch context — the ribbon buttons and the main panel both update together.

Tip Only the tabs for your enabled features are visible. If you don't see the Restaurant or HR tab, go to Home → Tools → Feature Management and enable the relevant feature.

Dashboard KPIs

The Home dashboard shows live key performance indicators at the top:

  • Today's Sales — total revenue for today
  • Transactions — number of completed sales today
  • Products — total products in your catalog
  • Customers — total registered customers
  • Cash Flow — net cash movement (income minus expenses)
  • Expenses — outgoing costs for the current period

KPIs refresh every time you open the Home tab or click Refresh in the Overview group.

Sales Analytics Chart

Below the KPIs is a bar chart showing daily sales over the past 14 days. Hover over any bar to see the exact amount for that day. Use this to spot weekly patterns and peak trading days.

Quick Actions

The ribbon's Quick Actions group gives one-click access to the most common tasks:

ButtonWhat it does
New SaleOpens the POS tab ready to start a sale
New ProductOpens the Add Product form in Inventory
New BillOpens the Create Bill form in Finance
New EmployeeOpens the Add Employee form in HR

Real-time Activity Feed

The right panel of the Home dashboard shows a live feed of recent events — new sales, stock adjustments, new customers, and system actions — updated automatically every 30 seconds alongside the background sync engine.

CHAPTER 03

Point of Sale

Process sales quickly, manage your cart, handle multiple sessions, and check out customers with cash, card, or bank transfer.

The POS Layout

The POS screen has three zones:

  • Left — Product grid: All your products displayed as cards, filterable by category
  • Right — Cart: Items added to the current sale, with totals and payment controls
  • Top — Search & category bar: Filter products by name, SKU, or barcode, and jump between categories

Starting a Sale

1
Open the POS tab

Click the POS ribbon tab, or press F2 from anywhere in the app.

2
Find a product

Click a product card directly, or type in the search bar to filter by name, SKU, or scan a barcode with F3. Click the Recent chip to show your most frequently sold items first.

3
Add to cart

Click any product card to add one unit. Click it again to increase quantity, or edit the quantity number directly in the cart. Remove a line by clicking the ✕ icon.

4
Apply a discount (optional)

If the Discount field is enabled in Settings, a discount box appears in the cart footer. Enter a percentage or fixed amount. This applies to the whole sale.

5
Process payment

Click Checkout. Select the payment method — Cash, Card, or Bank Transfer. For cash, enter the amount tendered and the change is calculated automatically. Click Confirm Sale.

6
Print or share receipt

After confirming, the receipt preview opens. Print directly or close to return to the POS.

Multiple POS Sessions (Tabs)

You can run several parallel sales at once — useful for holding an order while serving another customer. Each session is called a tab.

  • Click + New Tab in the POS session bar to open an additional register
  • Click any tab to switch between active sessions
  • Each tab holds an independent cart — switching tabs does not affect the other session's items

Parking an Order

If a customer needs to come back, click Park Order (in the POS ribbon group) to save the current cart without completing the sale. The parked order count badge appears in the ribbon. Click Parked Orders to retrieve and continue any parked sale.

Customers

Attach a customer to a sale for purchase history tracking and account management.

1
Search or create a customer

Click Customer in the cart header. Type a name or phone number to search existing customers, or click New Customer to add one on the spot.

2
Link to the sale

Select the customer from the dropdown. Their name appears in the cart and the sale is recorded in their purchase history.

Access the full customer list from the ribbon's Customers group to view purchase history, outstanding credits, and account balances.

Quotations

Create a quotation (price estimate) without completing a sale:

1
Build the cart as normal

Add the products and quantities the customer is interested in.

2
Click Quotation in the ribbon

Under the Quotations ribbon group, click New Quotation. The current cart items are saved as a quotation with a quote number.

3
Print or convert

Open the quotation from the Sales tab to print it or convert it to a completed sale.

CHAPTER 04

Sales & Returns

View your transaction history, handle customer returns, run end-of-day settlement, and manage sales invoices.

Transaction History

Click the SALES ribbon tab, then click Transactions in the ribbon to see all completed sales. Use the search bar to find a sale by number, customer name, or amount.

Click any transaction row to open its full detail — items sold, payment method, amounts, and receipt reprint options.

Sales History & Analytics

Click Sales History in the Transactions group to open the analytics view. Here you can:

  • Filter by date range to see any period's performance
  • See top-selling products in the period
  • View a daily bar chart of revenue
  • See total sales, total transactions, and average order value

Processing a Return

1
Open the Returns group

In the SALES ribbon, click New Return.

2
Find the original sale

Search by sale number or customer. Select the sale you're returning items from.

3
Select items and quantities

Check the items being returned and enter the quantity. The refund amount calculates automatically.

4
Select a return reason and confirm

Choose a reason from the list (Defective, Wrong item, Customer changed mind, etc.) and click Process Return. Stock is automatically restocked.

End-of-Day Settlement

At the end of each trading day, run a settlement to reconcile cash and clear the session. This is found in the Settlement ribbon group.

1
Click End-of-Day

The system tallies all transactions since the last settlement — cash sales, card payments, and bank transfers separately.

2
Count your cash drawer

Enter the physical cash amount in the drawer. The system shows the expected amount and any variance.

3
Confirm and print

Click Confirm Settlement. A settlement report is generated and the session resets for the next day.

Sales Invoices

Create and send formal invoices to business customers from Transactions → Invoices. Each invoice can include payment terms, due dates, and line-item descriptions. Invoices can be printed or exported as PDF.

CHAPTER 05

Inventory & Products

Build your product catalog, track stock, manage suppliers and purchase orders, and print barcodes.

Adding a Product

1
Open Inventory

Click the INVENTORY tab, then click New Product in the Catalog ribbon group.

2
Fill in the Overview tab

Enter the Product name, Category, Brand, SKU, and Barcode. Mark it active to make it visible in the POS.

3
Set pricing

Go to the Pricing tab. Enter the Cost price and Selling price. You can optionally add tiered selling units (e.g. sell individually and by the dozen at different prices).

4
Set initial stock

Go to the Stock tab. Enter the Opening stock quantity and Low stock alert level. The app will warn you when stock falls below this threshold.

5
Add images

Go to the Images tab. Click Choose Image to open the image picker — you can upload from your computer, choose from the File Manager, or generate an AI image.

6
Add variants (optional)

Go to the Variants tab to create size/colour/style variations of the same product, each with its own SKU and price.

7
Save

Click Save Product. The product immediately appears in the POS grid.

Categories & Brands

Organise your catalog with categories and brands. In the Inventory ribbon's Catalog group:

  • Click Categories to add, rename, or reorder product categories
  • Click Brands to manage your product brands — useful for filtering in both POS and inventory views
  • Click Discounts to create reusable discount rules that can be applied at checkout

Stock Management

Track your inventory levels and make adjustments from the Stock ribbon group.

ActionWhen to use
Stock AuditCount physical stock and correct discrepancies. Creates a permanent audit record.
Stock AdjustmentAdd or remove stock manually — for wastage, loss, or corrections.
Stock TransferMove stock between branches of the same business.
Tip Every sale automatically deducts from stock. Every purchase order receipt automatically adds to stock. Manual adjustments create an audit trail with timestamps and user attribution.

Suppliers

Maintain a supplier directory in the Suppliers ribbon group. For each supplier, store their name, contact details, address, and default payment terms. Suppliers are then linked to purchase orders.

Purchase Orders

Manage your purchasing workflow from Inventory → Purchasing → Purchase Orders.

1
Create a Draft PO

Click New PO. Select a supplier, set the order date and expected delivery date, then add line items — each with product, quantity, and unit cost.

2
Place the order

Once the PO is ready, click Place Order. The status changes to Ordered. The PO is now locked from further editing.

3
Receive goods (GRN)

When goods arrive, open the PO and click Receive Goods. Enter the quantities actually received. A Goods Receipt Note (GRN) is created and stock levels are updated immediately.

Barcode Printing

Select one or more products in the inventory list, then click Print Barcodes in the Print ribbon group. Choose your label sheet size, set the quantity per product, and print directly to any connected label printer.

CHAPTER 06

Finance & Accounts

Monitor cash flow, manage bills and expenses, track loans, handle rental income, and maintain your financial accounts.

Financial Overview — Cash Flow

Click the FINANCE tab to open the finance panel. The default view is Cash Flow, showing:

  • Monthly income vs. expenses chart
  • Running account balances
  • Recent income and expense transactions
  • Net cash position for the current period

Use the date range selector to zoom into any period or compare months.

Bank Accounts

All payments flow through accounts. Go to the Finance panel and click Accounts in the sub-navigation. Here you can:

  • Add bank accounts, cash tills, and mobile money wallets
  • View the full transaction ledger for each account
  • Transfer money between accounts
  • Reconcile account balances with your bank statement
Note You must add at least one bank account before you can process sales. The Bank Account Setup Wizard runs automatically on first login.

Bill Management

Record supplier invoices and bills you need to pay. In the Finance ribbon, click New Bill.

1
Enter bill details

Select the supplier, enter the invoice number, date, and due date. Add line items for each expense category.

2
Record payment

When you pay the bill, open it and click Record Payment. Select the account the payment was made from and the amount paid.

3
Track partial payments

You can make multiple partial payments against one bill. The outstanding balance updates after each payment until the bill is fully settled.

Loans

Track business loans — both money borrowed and money lent. From the Finance panel sub-navigation, click Loans. Record the principal amount, interest rate, and repayment schedule. The system tracks each repayment and shows the remaining balance.

Rentals & Properties

If your business manages rental properties or pays rent, the Rentals and Properties views let you track lease terms, monthly payments, and property-related expenses. Each property links to a billing schedule that reminds you of upcoming payments.

Finance Reports

Access standard financial reports from the Reports ribbon group:

  • Profit & Loss — income vs. expenses for any period
  • Account Statement — full ledger for a specific account
  • Expense Summary — breakdown of spending by category

Click New Employee

In the HR ribbon, click New Employee in the People group.

2
Overview tab — Basic information

Enter the employee's name, job title, email, phone, and start date. Assign them to a department and set their employment type (Full-time, Part-time, or Contract).

3
Personal tab — Identity details

Add the national ID number, date of birth, address, and emergency contact information.

4
Save the record

Click Save Employee. The employee now appears in your team roster and can be included in payroll runs.

Departments

Click Departments in the HR ribbon to create and manage departments. Each department can have:

  • A department head employee
  • A list of assigned employees
  • A salary sheet tab showing all members' compensation

Salary & Payroll

From the Compensation ribbon group, manage salaries and run payroll:

1
Set salary for each employee

Open an employee record and go to the salary section. Enter their base salary and any allowances.

2
Run payroll

Click Payroll Run. Select the pay period (monthly, bi-weekly), choose the employees to include, and confirm the amounts. The payroll is recorded as an expense against the business account you select.

3
Print payslips

After confirming, generate and print individual payslips for each employee from the payroll run summary screen.

Attendance

Track attendance directly in the app. Open an employee record and use the attendance log to record daily check-in and check-out times, late arrivals, absences, and leave. Attendance data feeds into payroll calculations automatically when configured.

How to Create a Letterhead

A professional letterhead gives your printed quotes, invoices, and formal letters a polished, branded look. Follow the steps below to design yours in the Design Studio.

Before you start Have your company logo image ready in the File Manager. Go to File Manager and upload the logo PNG/JPG first so it's available during design.
1 Open Design Studio
Click the DESIGN tab in the ribbon. If you don't see it, go to Home → Tools → Feature Management and enable Social Campaigns, then restart the app.
2 Create a New Flyer (A4)
Click New Design in the ribbon. Select Flyer as the design type — this uses A4 dimensions (595 × 842 pt) which is the standard for letterhead. Give your design the name Company Letterhead and click Create.
3 Add Your Company Logo
Click Add Image in the toolbar, then choose File Manager to pick your uploaded logo. Drag it to the top-left corner of the canvas. Resize by dragging the corner handles — aim for about 80–120 px height. Keep it proportional by holding Shift while resizing.
4 Add Your Business Name
Click Add Text. A text box appears — type your business name. In the text toolbar, set the font size to 22–26 pt, weight to Bold, and colour to your brand's primary colour. Position it to the right of or below the logo.
5 Add Contact Details
Click Add Text again. Type your contact block on separate lines:
123 Business Street, City, Country
Phone: +1 555 000 0000
Email: hello@yourbusiness.com
Website: www.yourbusiness.com
Set font size to 9–10 pt, colour to a mid-grey. Align it to match the business name block.
6 Draw a Dividing Line
Click Add Shape → Line. Draw a horizontal line spanning the full width of the page just below the header block. Set line thickness to 1–2 pt. Use your brand colour or a neutral dark grey. This cleanly separates the header from the letter body.
7 Add a Footer (Optional)
Scroll to the bottom of the canvas. Add a thin line near the very bottom, then add a small text block below it with your registration number, VAT/Tax ID, or a tagline. Use 8 pt font, centred or left-aligned. This gives official letters a complete, professional finish.
8 Save to Your Library
Click Save in the toolbar. The letterhead is stored in your Designs library under the name you gave it. You can reopen and edit it any time — useful when your address or contact details change.
9 Export as PDF or Image
Click Export. Choose PDF for print-quality output you can attach to emails or send to a printer. Choose PNG if you need a high-resolution image to overlay on documents in another app. The file is saved to your Downloads folder.
Design tips for a great letterhead
  • Keep the header height under 25% of the page — leave enough space for letter content.
  • Use no more than 2 fonts: one for the company name, one for contact details.
  • Stick to 2–3 brand colours maximum. Too many colours look unprofessional.
  • Ensure the logo has a transparent or white background so it blends cleanly.
  • Print a test copy before finalising — colours on screen often differ from print.
Using your letterhead with invoices Once exported as a PDF, your letterhead can be used as a background layer in any PDF editor. Alternatively, upload the exported PNG to the File Manager and use it as a background image in other Design Studio templates.

CHAPTER 09

Restaurant Management

Set up tables and floor plans, build your menu, manage dine-in orders, run the Kitchen Display System, and control ingredients.
Requirement The RESTAURANT and RESTAURANT POS tabs only appear when the Restaurant feature is enabled. This feature cannot be combined with Point of Sale.

Setting Up Tables

Before taking orders you need to create your tables. Click the RESTAURANT tab, then Tables in the ribbon's Floor group.

1
Click New Table

Enter the table Name (e.g. "Table 1", "Window Booth") and its Capacity (number of seats). Click Save.

2
Arrange the floor plan

In the Floor Plan view, drag tables to match your physical layout. The visual map helps staff navigate quickly.

3
Table statuses

Tables show as Available (green), Occupied (amber), or Reserved (blue). Status updates automatically when orders are created or completed.

Building Your Menu

Menu management lives in the Menu ribbon group.

1
Create Menu Categories first

Click Menu Categories and create categories like Starters, Mains, Desserts, and Drinks. Drag to reorder them — this order appears in the Restaurant POS grid.

2
Add Menu Items

Click Menu Items → New Item. Enter the name, category, price, and optional image. Use the Dietary tab to mark items as Vegetarian, Vegan, Gluten-Free, etc.

3
Link ingredients

Go to the Ingredients tab of each menu item and assign the ingredients used and the quantity per serving. This powers automatic stock deduction when dishes are ordered.

4
Toggle availability

Use the toggle on each menu item to mark it as available or unavailable. Unavailable items are hidden from the Restaurant POS grid in real time.

Managing Orders

The Orders view shows all current and historical orders with their statuses. Filter by status — Pending, Preparing, Ready, Served, or Paid.

Click any order to open its detail panel, where you can update item statuses individually, add notes, or cancel the order.

StatusMeaning
PendingOrder placed, not yet started in the kitchen
PreparingKitchen has started cooking
ReadyFood is ready at the pass — waiting to be served
ServedAll items delivered to the table
PaidBill settled — table marked available

Kitchen Display System (KDS)

The KDS is a live ticket board designed to run on a screen in the kitchen. Open it by clicking Kitchen Display in the KDS ribbon group.

  • Each order appears as a card with all its items listed
  • Kitchen staff click items to mark them as Preparing, then Ready
  • Cards disappear from the KDS once the order is paid
  • When new items are added to a preparing order, they appear as a new separate card so kitchen staff can see exactly what's new
Tip Run the KDS on a dedicated screen or tablet in the kitchen by opening a second window. The display polls for updates every 5 seconds.

Ingredient Management

Track raw ingredient stock from Restaurant → Ingredients. Each ingredient has a unit of measurement (kg, L, pcs), a current stock level, and a low-stock threshold.

When a menu item is ordered, its ingredient quantities are deducted automatically if you've linked them in the menu item's Ingredients tab.

Ingredient Purchase Orders

Order raw ingredients from your suppliers using the Purchase Order system under Restaurant → Purchase Orders. The workflow mirrors the inventory PO system:

  1. Create a Draft PO with supplier and line items (ingredients + quantities)
  2. Place the order — status changes to Ordered
  3. When goods arrive, receive them via a GRN — ingredient stock levels update immediately
CHAPTER 10

Restaurant POS

Take dine-in table orders and takeaway orders, track kitchen status in real time, and process payment when all dishes are served.

The Restaurant POS Interface

Click the RESTAURANT POS tab to open the order-taking screen. It has two modes, switchable from the ribbon:

  • Takeaway mode — orders without a table. Enter the customer's name and phone number.
  • Table mode — select a table from the floor plan to attach the order to that table.

The left side shows the menu grid (items grouped by category). The right side shows the cart / current order. Tabs across the top let you manage multiple active orders simultaneously.

Taking a Table Order

1
Select a table

Click Tables in the Restaurant POS ribbon. The floor plan opens — click an available table (shown in green). A new tab opens for that table.

2
Add items from the menu grid

Click menu item cards to add them to the order. Click a card multiple times to increase the quantity. All items are tracked with their kitchen status.

3
Send to kitchen

Click Send Order. The order is created in the system and immediately appears on the KDS in the kitchen. The table status changes to Occupied.

4
Track kitchen status

The cart shows each item's kitchen status — Pending → Preparing → Ready → Served. Statuses update in real time as kitchen staff mark items on the KDS. The live indicator in the top bar pulses to show the poll is active.

5
Add more items during service

If the order is still Pending, new items are added to the same kitchen ticket. If the kitchen has already started Preparing, new items create a new separate kitchen card automatically — so nothing gets missed.

6
Complete and process payment

The Complete Order button becomes active only when all ordered items have been marked as Served on the KDS. Click it to open the payment screen.

7
Select payment method and confirm

Choose Cash, Card, or Bank Transfer. For cash, enter the tendered amount. Click Confirm Payment. A receipt is printed, the order is marked Paid, and the table returns to Available.

Taking a Takeaway Order

1
Click Takeaway in the ribbon

A new tab opens with the customer name/phone fields.

2
Enter customer details and add items

Fill in the customer's name and phone (optional but useful for pickup notifications). Add menu items from the grid.

3
Send to kitchen and complete

Same flow as a table order — send, track on KDS, complete when all items are served/ready for collection.

Multiple Active Orders

Each tab at the top of the Restaurant POS represents an active order (a table or a takeaway). You can have as many tabs open as you need. Switching between tabs never loses your data — each tab's cart, order ID, and kitchen statuses are tracked independently.

Important Once the kitchen has started preparing an order (status: Preparing), you cannot increase the quantity of existing items. Add new quantities as separate items — they'll appear on a new KDS card.
CHAPTER 11

Feature Management

Enable or disable features at any time. Changes take effect immediately after the app reloads.

Opening Feature Management

Click the HOME tab → Tools group → Feature Management. A modal opens showing all available features with toggle switches.

Enabling and Disabling Features

1
Click a toggle to change its state

Blue (on) means the feature is enabled. Grey (off) means it's disabled. The toggle updates instantly in the modal.

2

Industry-Specific Defaults

When you choose Restaurant or Bar as your industry during registration, the Restaurant feature is automatically enabled and Point of Sale is disabled. You can change this at any time through Feature Management.

Toggling Dark Mode

You can also toggle dark mode quickly from the VIEW ribbon tab → Appearance group → Dark Mode. No restart required — the interface switches immediately.

Checking for Updates

Click your profile name (top-right) → Check for Updates. The app contacts the Zeebroo server and compares the installed version against the latest release. If an update is available, a download link is shown for your platform (Windows, macOS, or Linux).

Signing Out

Click your profile name (top-right) → Sign Out, or click Sign Out in the VIEW ribbon's Account group. Your local session clears and the login screen reappears. Your data on the server is never affected by signing out.

Important If you switch branches or businesses, any unsaved POS cart data will be lost. Ensure all active sales are completed or parked before switching.

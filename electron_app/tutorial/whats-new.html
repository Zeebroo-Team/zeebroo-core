Reading the Charts

The X-axis shows hours in 12-hour format (12 AM, 1 AM … 11 PM). The Y-axis auto-scales to the highest value in the visible range. Grid lines and axis labels appear automatically.

1
Early in the day

If you open the Today tab at 7 AM, only 7 data points (midnight through 6 AM) are drawn. Hours with no sales show as zero, which is normal.

2
Peak hours

The curve climbs during busy periods. The dot at the rightmost point marks the current hour — the most recent data point.

3
Refreshing

Click Refresh in the Today toolbar, or switch to another tab and back, to pull the latest hour's data from the server.

Note Charts use your screen's pixel density (devicePixelRatio) for crisp rendering on high-DPI displays. They also redraw automatically when you switch between light and dark mode.

How COGS Is Calculated

Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) is recorded at the moment of checkout, not retrospectively. When a cashier completes a sale, the system looks up the stock layer cost for each item and stores it in the sale record. This means:

  • Changing a product's cost after a sale has no effect on past COGS figures.
  • Products added to stock via Goods Receive Notes carry their GRN unit cost into the calculation.
  • If stock layer cost is not available (e.g. service items with no stock), that item contributes zero to COGS and its full selling price becomes gross profit.
Note Returns are tracked separately and excluded from the revenue and COGS totals shown in the Profit Report. The Returns amount is calculated but not currently displayed as a standalone KPI card.

Top Earners & Department View

The right-hand panel changes based on what data is available:

  • If a finalized cycle exists, the panel shows the Top 8 Earners from that cycle — ranked by net pay, with a bar indicating relative earnings and deduction amounts shown below.
  • If no finalized cycle exists yet, the panel falls back to a Department headcount view — listing each department with its employee count and a proportional bar.
